G7 Finance Ministers Reach Landmark Agreement on Global Minimum Tax Enforcement
By Clara Steinberg, Economics Correspondent · Published today
Finance ministers from the seven largest economies signed a binding protocol on Sunday that commits member states to enforcing the 15 percent global minimum corporate tax agreed in 2021, closing loopholes that had allowed multinationals to continue shifting profits to low-tax jurisdictions.
The deal is expected to raise an estimated €200 billion in additional tax revenue annually across G7 nations, funds earmarked primarily for green infrastructure and digital public services.
